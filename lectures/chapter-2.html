2.1 OSI Layer Model

At the beginning of the computer networks (1980's) all companies had different ways to communicate their computers.
As the network become bigger and bigger, the world realized how serious will be the incompatibility.

Then, the concept of network protocol was born.
A network protocol is a list of rules that allow two or more computers transmit information.
These rules defines the syntax, semantics and synchronization of the information and possible error recovery methods.

The OSI (Open System Interconnection) Model is the standard conceptual model of the today computer communications.
This model partitions a communication in abstract layers.
Each layer have different network protocols.
